Request for duplicate statement can be raised in CBCI for the last 9 statement generation months.


If customer requests for statements which is more than 9 months, the below mentioned process to be followed:


PhoneBanker should inform customer that only e-statements will be sent on the registered email id for duplicate statements earlier to 9 months
No physical statements request to be taken.
Only if no email id is updated on the card account can the user take a physical duplicate statement request.
All other request for Physical duplicate statement will not be actioned but rejected. This process is live from 3rd April 2017


For cases where customers are requesting for duplicate physical statements / e-statements which is not available in CBCI (> 9 months only), the PhoneBanker will need to raise a referral using the below mentioned sub-category in CBCI.


FILTER                       – STATEMENT AND PAYMENT
CATEGORY              – DUPLICATE STATEMENTS
SUB-CATEGORY    – DUPLICATE STMT THRU NEWGEN
MAIN PAGE
These cases will get auto referred to PhoneBanking Central team at Chennai who will perform the below actions.


FOR DUPLICATE PHYSICAL STATEMENTS
•           On receiving the CBCI referral, the PhoneBanking Central team agent at Chennai will retrieve the required statements from Newgen, print them and will arrange for the same to be dispatched to the customers mailing address as available on the card account.
•           Referrals raised on day 0 will be actioned on day 1.  No exceptions will be entertained for intraday actioning. Hence maximum TAT for receiving the referrals and actioning the Physical duplicate statement copy request will be 2 working days.


DUPLICATE E-STATEMENTS
•           On receiving the CBCI referral, the PhoneBanking Central team agent at Chennai agent will retrieve the required statements from Newgen and will save them as soft copies and will forward the e-statement copies to the email team at QIG correspondence team through mail.
•           Referrals raised on day 0 will be actioned on day 1.  No exceptions will be entertained for intraday actioning.
•           Back-end team will forward an e-mail to the customer with these e-statements at attachments to the e-mail with a 1 day TAT thereon. Hence maximum TAT for receiving the referrals and actioning the email duplicate statement copy request will be 2 working days.


IMPORTANT


  • Duplicate Statements on call can be requested only by the primary card holder.
  • E-statement mode must be selected only after checking if the account has an e-mail address maintained and the email will be sent to the E-statement flag maintained in the CBCI system.
  • If customer is requesting for both physical copy and a soft copy to be dispatched, then the team member can trigger a duplicate statement request through the CURRENT MAILING ADDRESS option which will send out both physical statement and e-mail statement.
  • For customers who are requesting for a duplicate physical statement copy, the team member must inform the customer that a physical copy as well as a soft copy will be dispatched to them.
  • Duplicate statement requests cannot be processed basis requests received from add on cardholders even if they are able to clear level checks or AOC. 
  • Duplicate statement requests can be processed only post level 2 checks / AOC are cleared by the primary cardholder.
  • Duplicate statement requests cannot be processed basis request received from the HDFC Bank branches for their internal review processes.
  • Duplicate statement requests can be processed basis request received from HDFC Bank branches only, if the same was requested by the customer through the branch. In this case the duplicate statement will get dispatched to the customers registered mailing address or e-mail id.

E-STATEMENT REGISTRATION PROCESS

The process to be followed when a primary card holder calls customer services for account balance related is mentioned below -

  • Team member must access the account by selecting PRIMARY from the card summary screen.
  • Once in the account, the COMMON STATIC INFORMATION & the sub-links in the ACCOUNT DETAILS screen will be displayed.
  • The team member can check the below mentioned fields in the COMMON STATIC INFORMATION screen to check if the account is already registered for e-statements.
    • Statement Regi. Type : 
    • E-Statement Flag:                           MAIN PAGE
  • To proceed with the e-statement registration, the team member will need to click on the SDM link from the menu bar & then select E-STATEMENT (CS) sub-link.
  • The system will now prompt for required validation. The customer will need to clear required validation as maintained in CBCI for the team member to proceed with the e-statement process.
  • On clearing the Validation the SDM – E-statement screen will come up with 5 web-form fields.
    • Type of request.
    • Statement Flag.
    • Email Flag.
    • Email ID #1 (Off.)
    • Email ID #2 (Res.)
  • In the Type of request web-form field by default the option will be populated as registration. This is the case only if the account is already not registered for e-statements.
  • If the account is already registered for e-statements than the field will be pre-populated as Deregistration. 
  • In the statement flag field the drop down options available are -
    • E-mail.
    • Both (Email & paper)
  • In the Email flag field the drop down options available are -
    • Residence           
    • Office
    • Both (Off & Res)
  • Then click on the UPDATE tab. The details will now get updated instantly.


  •  Whenever PhoneBanker register for e-Statement registration, PhoneBanker to select Email flag as either OFFICE or RESIDENCE. PhoneBanker not to select BOTH in the EMAIL FLAG field.




PHYSICAL STATEMENT SUPPRESSION PROCESS


Physical statement suppression is proposed to be promoted by the bank. Hence customers will be provided with offers to register for Physical Statement Suppression. To start off, a base of cards have been identified as suitable for Physical Statement suppression. These card accounts will be populated with PHY STMT SUPPRESSION eligibility in the Eligible products link.                                              

                                             MAIN PAGE


  • For these eligible card accounts, customers will benefit with 200 reward points for opting for physical statement suppression.
  • Before communicating on the physical statement suppression, the team member will need to check the ‘Statement Regi. Type’ flag if E. (E means physical statement is suppressed). If yes, then do not communicate on the statement suppression and the reward points.
  • If the ‘Statement Regi. Type’ flag is P or B, then PBK to communicate to the customers regarding the physical statement suppression and the 200 reward points.
  • Where ever the customer is interested for physical statement suppression, the team member must ensure positive level 1, 2 & 3 check validations with the customer.

Post that, team member can raise the below referral in CBCI through Case Management.

FILTER                                 - STATEMENT AND PAYMENT

CATEGORY              - ORIGINAL STATEMENTS

SUB-CATEGORY    - PHYSICAL STMT SUPPRESSION REQ


User will need to select ‘Yes’ in the PHYSICAL STMT SUPPRESSION REQ webform field and then submit the cases. The case will get registered on the card account and will remain in closed status. (Not a referral to Ops)

User will need to inform the customer that physical statement suppression will be enabled on the card account only from next calendar month onwards. Suppression of the physical statements and applicable reward points crediting to the card account will be done within the 1st week of the following calendar month.

IMPORTANT –

  • Do not action physical Statement suppression though SDM > E-statement(CS) link.
MAIN PAGE
  • Cases raised on card accounts which are not eligible for physical statement suppression will not be actioned.
  • These accounts will be updated with memos using CATEGORY ELIGIBILITY UPDATE & SUB CATEGORY - PHYSICAL STMT SUPPRESSION which can be referenced later on post the eligibility being dropped off the card accounts.
  • Statement flag will be changed to B flag if the customer moves to delinquent bucket in future.
  • Stmt suppression is not possible via net banking.

REVISION IN CREDIT CARD PAYMENT GRACE DAYS
                                        
STRICTLY INTERNAL – not for customer communication.


As a revision to this grace period, the number of grace days is now being changed as 3 days instead of 4 days. Hence the late payment charges will be levied to the card account where the payments have not been credited to the card account by the end of the 3rd grace days. (PDD + 3 days). This change will be effective for the statements which will be generated from August 2013 onwards.


While this is internal information to the bank and is not communicated to the customers, customers how have made payments post the due date may have noticed this and understood that the system allows the 4 days grace period. Hence where ever the payment
 is received on the 4th grace day, going forward these cases will also be levied with the late payment charges.
When these customers contact us regarding being charged with Late payment charges, please inform the customer to make payments such that the payment credit hits the card accounts by the required due date as mentioned in the statements. Do not communicate any details regarding the earlier grace days or on the revised grace days.

MAD CALCULATION


Effective 1st SEPTMEBER 2014, Minimum amount due will be calculated as 5% of Total Amount Due* (or) Over limit amount whichever is higher will be billed as Minimum Amount Due to customers.


Note :


Existing EMI on credit card such as Insta loan, Jumbo loan, smart emi will be part of the MAD payment.
                             
As per the old process, the MAD has been calculated as-5% of Total Amount Due* + Over limit amount will be communicated as Minimum Amount Due to customers.


* 5% will be calculated on the total outstanding (inclusive of over limit amount).

DEBIT AND CREDIT ADJUSTMENT IN CTD



Whenever customer makes excess payment as per his last generated

statement, the excess payment will be moved to cash plan. When customer

incur any retail transactions system will do internal adjustment between

cash plan and retail plan (i.e. Debit the transaction amount from cash plan and

credit the same in retail plan).


As a system functionality,all the debits & credit amounts  (whether purchase transactions or adjustments) to be accounted on the card account. for system reconciliation.


The credit adjustment amount will be displayed in CBCI & NetBanking.


If customer calling to know the details of the Total debits and Total credits in NetBanking, customer call to be handled accordingly.
